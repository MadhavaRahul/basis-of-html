JALEBI RECIEPI BY MMR


Making jalebi, a popular Indian dessert, involves preparing the dough, making the sugar syrup, and frying the jalebis. Here are detailed steps for making jalebi with filler:

Ingredients:

  • For Jalebi Dough:

    • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
    • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder (for color)
    • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
    • 1 cup yogurt
    • Water as needed
    • Ghee or oil for frying

  • For Sugar Syrup:

    • 1 cup sugar
    • 1/2 cup water
    • 1/4 teaspoon saffron strands (optional)
    • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon lemon juice

  • For Filling:

    • 1 cup khoya (milk solids)
    • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
    • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
    • 2 tablespoons chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios, etc.)

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Jalebi Dough

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, turmeric powder, baking powder, and baking soda.
  2. Add Wet Ingredients: Add yogurt to the dry mixture and mix well. Add water gradually to make a smooth batter. The batter should be of pouring consistency but not too runny.
  3. Fermentation: Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours or overnight.

Step 2: Prepare the Sugar Syrup

  1. Boil the Syrup: In a saucepan, add sugar and water. Bring to a boil while stirring occasionally until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Flavoring: Add saffron strands (if using), cardamom powder, and lemon juice. Boil the syrup until it reaches a one-string consistency. Keep the syrup warm.

Step 3: Prepare the Filling

  1. Khoya: In a pan, cook the khoya on low heat until it softens and slightly melts.
  2. Add Flavoring: Add powdered sugar, cardamom powder, and chopped nuts. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Let it cool down.

Step 4: Shape and Fry the Jalebis

  1. Prepare a Piping Bag: Pour the fermented batter into a piping bag or a squeeze bottle with a small nozzle.
  2. Heat the Oil: Heat ghee or oil in a wide pan on medium heat.
  3. Shape Jalebis: Squeeze the batter into the hot oil in circular motions to form spiral shapes. Fry until golden and crisp on both sides.
  4. Soak in Syrup: Remove the fried jalebis from the oil and immediately dip them into the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for a few seconds, then remove and place on a plate.

Step 5: Fill the Jalebis

  1. Pipe the Filling: Using a piping bag with a small nozzle, fill the prepared khoya mixture into the jalebis. Make a small hole in the jalebis if necessary to pipe the filling inside.

Step 6: Serve

  1. Garnish: Optionally, garnish the filled jalebis with more chopped nuts or saffron strands.
  2. Serve: Serve warm or at room temperature.`
Enjoy your homemade jalebis with a delicious filling!
